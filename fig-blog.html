First up was the Delphine skirt from Tilly and the Buttons "Love at First Stitch" book. I made this on a whim as I had spare wool fabric left over from the Chloe coat class but I am completely in love with this skirt. It was one of those tetris-style cutting out sessions as I only had just enough fabric left.
I found myself coveting a lot of wool skirts this winter so I am really pleased I made myself one and in an unusual colour.
I wore this to afternoon tea recently and again to a birthday meal with the parentals.  My heart nearly broke when I saw the zip had broken on the second wearing! I really took my time to match up the waistbands and insert it correctly. üíî
It had split below the zip pull and I couldn't unzip it. Luckily with a lot patience, I managed to get the zip back to the bottom and can gently zip it back up again.  However I am now left with a very annoying step at the top - any tips on how to correct this? 

 Time Taken: 3 hours sewing time
Fabric: Raspberry Melton Wool, fabworks.com
Skills included: interfacing waistband, inserting invisible zip
Other comments: I graded this pattern out from a T&TB size 5 to 6 from waist to hips.  I found on this skirt and my previous Delphine's, however, that it sticks out at the hips, perhaps I didn't grade it gently enough. 
Next up, Coco top (again Tilly and the Buttons).  This fabric is so super soft I found myself wearing the remnants around me as a blanket whilst sewing. 
This is my 4th Coco top and 6th overall Coco make. Not as many as a lot of Tilly fans but the collection is growing slowly.
The added bonus is that I can wear these two together - wahoo, whole new outfit. 
Time taken: 1hour cutting out, 2 hours sewing
Fabric: denim and oatmeal stripe French Terry knit, Girl Charlee UK
Skills included: stabilising shoulders, inserting sleeves
Bonus sew this month was a Sew Over It 'Ultimate Shift Dress'.  I saw this embroidered flamingo fabric on Minerva Crafts website and knew it would make a great birthday dress.  This dress was super quick to make. I made version one with three quarter sleeves.
Time taken: 40 mins cutting out, 3 and a half hours sewing
Fabric: embroidered linen-look cotton, Minerva Crafts
Skills included: inserting sleeves, hook and eye finishing
Other comments: I graded the pattern out to accommodate my lady hips and I had to lengthen it by 2 inches but I still feel it is quite short for my liking.
Also had to grade the sleeves out but still found I had to sew at 1cm seam allowance otherwise it was too tight around the bingo wings.
The sleeves are gathered at the top and I think due to the fabric being very stable that instead of neat gathers its turned more into pleats.
